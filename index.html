A DAY IN ST LUCIA

When you're in St. Lucia, there are a lot of things to do, for example a hippo cruise, fetching some freshly picked pineapple or buying handmade souvenirs for your friends at home. Because of the small size of the town, it is very easy to get aroung by foot. But be aware of the wild animals that might cross your path! Not only crocodiles but also hippos love to roam around town, especially once the sun has set.

And one thing's for sure: You don't want to bump into a hippo in the middle of the night. The streets are quiet and very close to the estuary. So the chances of seeing a hippo walking across the street is very hight, if you're staying one night or more in this beautiful little coastal town. Looking at the size of a hippo, you might be thinking that you'd be able to outrun it. But trust me, you don't want to take that chance.

Those clumsy looking mammals can run up to 48km/h on land! No offense, but I don't think you can beat that.

The moral of the story: At night you should always bring a torch and be aware of what's happening around you. If you do that, you will be perfectly fine. You will definetely not be the only person walking around, St. Lucia is becoming more and more popular and the locals love it. So if you want to experience St. Lucia like a local, I've got a tip for you: Go to the little Fish & Chips restaurant next to the fuel station and walk right to the back of it. You will find yourself in a local pub that serves ice cold beer. And if you'd like, you might as well grab some fresh Fish & Chips on the way.


Lost in Paradise

Mozambique, a small country with beautiful white beaches, the warm waves of the indian ocean and lots of friendliness. Ponta do Ouro is one of the beautiful little towns that will make you fall in love with this part of Africa.

The first time I went to Mosambique, I went straight to Ponta do Ouro, a town right behind the border between South Africa and Mosambique. It didn't take me long to realizle how beautiful and special this place is. The people are soft-hearted and welcoming, the streets are mostly made of sand and the bush is wild and lush.

There are so many wonderful places where you can stay the night but one of my absolute favorites is the DEVOCEAN Eco Adventure Lodge. They offer tented accomodation but you can choose between three categories of tents – from small to medium to luxurious, with your very own bathroom. You will find it in the center of town, not far from the bars, little restaurants and the beach.



Cape Town, Baby!

Cape Town, the Mother City, colorful, loud, bold. Some love her, some hate her. I personally am obsessed with it. When you are in Cape Town the best thing you can do is just to join the vibe of the city. Try and stay a few days if possible so that you can enjoy the full experience. Obviously there are things that you would find on almost every tourists bucket list:

  • Enjoy the view on table mountain
  • Visit the penguins at Boulders Beach
  • Take instagrammable-photos in front of the colorful Bo-Kaap houses
And that is all good and well but there is a lot more to do. My travel tip No 1 also applies to your visit of the Mother City: Don't let other people's bucket lists dictate your programm. It's okay to have been in Cape Town without having been on top of Table Mountain. It's also okay not to spend 10€ to get as close as possible to the famous penguins. You do you. Enjoy the ride. This is Cape Town, Baby.
